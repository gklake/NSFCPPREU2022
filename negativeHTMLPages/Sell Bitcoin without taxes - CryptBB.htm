A great thing about cryptocurrency becoming mainstream is that there are more and more legitimate routes for it to be utilized.

Even since the near beginning of bitcoin, there were physical exchanges that would take cash for btc and vice versa.

Wherever you're located, try finding a service that allows you to exchange bitcoin for cash in-person. Of course, you're less likely to find trade deals for larger and larger sums of currency and you will most definitely sell at a rate below market price but assuming you're being truthful about living in Canada there's probably enough incentive to do so anyway. The real cost for all of this is convenience and time.

You're off to a good start getting your coins off big KYC exchanges, since they all report transactions to the taxman now. As Pronoxys mentioned, your best bet is to sell the BTC for cash either in person or using a service like localbitcoins where the buyer will send you fiat by mail, or a peer-to-peer exchange like Bisq. If you want to retain plausible deniability, swapping BTC for XMR first and then selling the XMR for cash would make it harder to track the flow. In your situation it's worth the extra cost. BTC is trivial to trace and you don't want to end up paying taxes and whatever other penalties if they catch you.
