Protonmail can't scan your emails if they're end-to-end encrypted.
they support PGP encryption as well so that's not a problem. they're more trust worthy as they're a legit company and they are open source.
it is true tho that they can send the data of which IPs you used to log into an account if Swiss agency asks them (or if another country wants to and they do coop)
but if you only use their onion address and access your email through Tor only (or a VPN) then there's nothing to do with that IP address.

potentially you can use any email provider you want if you PGP encrypt your messages. but if you don't - then it comes to how you trust each company.
if they're open source it's better. if they're a legit company that requires to report cases like if they disclose user information or something like that even better.
