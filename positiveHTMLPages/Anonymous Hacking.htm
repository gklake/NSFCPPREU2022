Go with proxychains on github, it's very useful for anything hacking related becuase you can structure how your proxies work.

You can install it with:
Code:
sudo apt install proxychains

You can access the configuration file at:
Code:
/etc/proxychains.conf

It can proxify through TOR, Socks4, Socks5, HTTPS and HTTP. And you can even use it in conjunction with other applications like:
Code:
proxychains firefox google.com
or
Code:
proxychains nmap -sT 127.0.0.1

Hope this helps!

You need a good understanding of operational security, there are great videos about that subject on YouTube, check them out, all of them (opsec failure)
learn about the differences between privacy, anonymity and security, there is a ton of techniques for anonymity, it really depends on what type of hacking you are performing, and how you are performing it

A lot of things can kill your anonymity, but the first thing you have to consider is your IP address.

You could use Tor and proxys to avoid the IP ban, you could connect to a VPS via ssh, you could hack a local WiFi and use their IP, you could use an open WiFi
It really depend on what you need

After that, think about your MAC address, what if you are using a public WiFi, and LE trace it back ?
What if they look at the log and get your MAC, what if you have buy your hardware in a non-anonymous fashion, is there a money trail that LE could use ?
And if they sit there to analyze the traffic, what happen next time you connect with the same MAC ? Could they catch you ? (all of that is pretty unlikely for everyday hacks, but you never know)

And what about motherboard and HDD ids ?
What if you get a malware on your pc that can de-anonymize you ?
A malware that reveal your real IP address ?

You get the point, there is a lot to consider, and even if some of these techniques can be consider overkill, you never know when someone's gonna take interest in you.
A good solution to all of that could be to use a VM with a Whonix gateway, all your traffic would go trough tor, your ID and MAC address wouldn't be link to you, thanks to the nature of a VM, and if you use non-persistence or snapshot, you could even be sure to never leave logs or malware on your device, ip and dns leak would be impossible (if you use the whonix model), etc.

There is a lot to consider, again, the first thing is your IP address
