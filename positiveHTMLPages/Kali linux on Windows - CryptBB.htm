For performing real world attacks it's absolutely not a good idea unless you want to go in jail.

an important thing that often gets overlooked: encryption.

that indeed is a good start, using kali live in a VM and paired with VPNs is sufficiently safe to do small scale stuff. but you must encrypt your host machine, because even through virtualization your host machine can be infected through windows exploits. i personally encrypt all my drives separately, as well as the operating system. its a bitch having to manage over a dozen passwords but at least i know if for any reason I am compromised, all my data is protected behind several layers of encryption.
